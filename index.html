Mastering the tooltip

This workshop was imparted at the d3 unconf in Chicago on the 26th August of 2019.

What is a tooltip? In its simplest form it is a box that floats on top of the content providing additional context. A tooltip is usually hidden by default and toggled by the user with a certain action. We use them in interactive charts to expose data points to the reader.

If we look back at the history of writing, humans have been annotating, commenting and adding context to the written word for millenia. Whereas marginalia is usually added by the reader and not by the original author its intention is similar to what a good tooltip should strive to be. The brain always seeks out further context and additional datum.

In this workshop we will start with the simplest tooltip and work our way through various techniques.
